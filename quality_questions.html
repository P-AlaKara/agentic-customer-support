Quality Evaluation Questions

LLM-as-a-Judge Framework for Agentic Customer Support System

Evaluation Approach: These 12 questions are designed for LLM-as-a-judge evaluation. Use these prompts to have an LLM evaluate system responses across multiple quality dimensions: intent recognition accuracy, sentiment analysis appropriateness, policy compliance, response relevance, routing decisions, empathy, and escalation necessity. Each question targets a specific aspect of your multi-agent customer support system.
1
Did the system correctly identify the customer's intent (e.g., returns, shipping, onboarding) with high confidence?
Evaluate whether the intent agent properly classified the customer's request. Should assess confidence scores and whether the classification was appropriate for the context.
Intent Recognition
2
Was the customer's sentiment (neutral, positive, negative, angry) accurately detected and appropriately factored into the response strategy?
Judge whether sentiment analysis was accurate and whether the response tone matched the detected sentiment. Consider if escalation was appropriate for negative/angry sentiment.
Sentiment Analysis
3
Was the customer routed to the correct agent/business process (returns, shipping, onboarding, etc.) based on their intent?
Evaluate the routing decision made by the coordinator. Assess if the customer was directed to the appropriate specialized agent for their specific need.
Routing Accuracy
4
Did the response accurately cite and follow the relevant policy (returns, shipping, onboarding) for the customer's request?
Check if the business process agent correctly applied the relevant policy document. Verify that policy details were accurate and correctly integrated into the response.
Policy Compliance
5
Is the response factually accurate and grounded in the retrieved context/knowledge base, or are there hallucinations or unsupported claims?
Evaluate RAG performance. Determine if the response is factually correct and properly supported by retrieved context, without fabrications or misleading information.
Factual Accuracy
6
Does the response directly address the customer's specific question and request with relevant, actionable information?
Assess whether the response is on-topic and provides useful, actionable guidance for the customer's situation. Judge relevance and usefulness.
Response Relevance
7
Is the tone empathetic, professional, and appropriate to the customer's sentiment and situation?
Evaluate the response tone. Judge if it conveys empathy where needed, maintains professionalism, and matches the customer's emotional state appropriately.
Tone & Empathy
8
Did the system appropriately recognize when escalation to a human operator was necessary?
Evaluate escalation logic. Judge if the system correctly identified situations requiring human intervention (low confidence, angry sentiment, complex issues) and escalated appropriately.
Escalation Logic
9
Is the response coherent, well-structured, and easy for the customer to understand and follow?
Assess response clarity and structure. Judge if the message is well-organized, uses clear language, and provides step-by-step guidance where needed.
Coherence & Clarity
10
Did the system maintain conversation context and reference relevant information from the customer's history or earlier messages?
Evaluate context awareness. Judge if the response incorporates prior conversation context appropriately and maintains coherence across the conversation flow.
Context Awareness
11
Does the response anticipate follow-up questions or provide proactive guidance to prevent future issues?
Evaluate proactivity. Judge if the response goes beyond the immediate question to help prevent future issues or provides guidance for next steps.
Proactivity
12
Is the overall response quality likely to improve customer satisfaction and resolve the issue effectively?
Holistic evaluation. Judge the overall quality and likelihood that the response will satisfy the customer and effectively resolve their issue without requiring escalation.
Overall Quality
Usage: Use these questions with an LLM evaluator to assess system responses. Each question can be scored on a scale (e.g., 1-5 or binary pass/fail) to generate quantitative quality metrics.
